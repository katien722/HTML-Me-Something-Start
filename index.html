Why Self-Care is Important...and Care-Stacking

Today I meditated, made my bed and danced to two songs before I brushed my teeth. These things are so important for my personal self-care plan.

I have realized the importance of focusing on the little ways to take care of myself so that I bring the best version of myself to all that I do on a daily basis. The way that I care for myself effects all aspects of my life, kind of like a game of Jenga.

I don't know which piece (or element of self-care, in this analogy) is going to cause the tower to fall (self to break-down), so I need to keep all pieces in 50-ways-to-practice-self-care for a strong foundation. And, just like in a game of Jenga, balance is key.

Balancing all parts of myself includes making sure that I tend to the physical, social, and emotional aspects of myself. Physically, I need to make sure that I feed and fuel my body effectively and use the gift of mobility that I am blessed with. Socially, I need to ensure positive interactions with my peers, because healthy connection with others contributes to my well-being.

Emotionally, I need to give myself opportunities to express my feelings, rest when needed, and take time to assess and regulate my emotions so that I may approach each situation, opportunity, and obstacle that presents itself efficiently and with a clear mind. What is care-stacking? I think I made up this term, but to me, it is a way to maximize my self-care habits. For example, making delicious and nutritious meals checks the boxes for creative expression (emotional need), nutrient-dense food (physical need), and sharing my creations with friends (social need)...I have stacked my self-care with one act that fulfills me in three aspects.
